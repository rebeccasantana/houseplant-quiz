Air Plant

Photo by epen2c on Flickr. Attribution 2.0 Generic (CC BY 2.0)

If you’re busy and you can’t set a lot of time aside to take care of your plants, an air plant is the perfect house plant for you. They prefer bright (but filtered) sunlight, so you can put them pretty much anywhere there’s a window nearby. They don’t take up a lot of space and only require your attention once a week. You don’t even need to worry about soil.

Philodendron

No matter where you live or how much light you have access to, a philodendron is a great starter plant. It requires some attention, so it's important that you check the soil frequently to make sure it doesn’t get too dry, but it shouldn’t need daily watering. This is a beautiful hanging plant, great for anyone who doesn’t have too much floor and/or desk space. It might benefit from some bi-yearly fertilization, but it’s not necessary.

Indoor Palm

If you’re looking to dive into house plants face-first and have some time on your hands, consider getting an indoor palm. Indoor palms are big, beautiful floor plants that will brighten any room in your home. They require a lot of bright light, warm temperatures and high humidity. Keep a close eye on these guys as they can be somewhat temperamental. If you can keep this plant alive, it’ll continue to grow bigger, bushier and more beautiful by the year. It might be a lot of work, but it’s totally worth it.

Artificial Plant

Photo by Marco Verch on Flickr. Attribution 2.0 Generic (CC BY 2.0)

Indoor horticulture probably isn’t the hobby for you. Any plant is going to require some time, space and energy to keep it alive. Plants can be expensive, so there’s no reason to by them if you’ll just let them wilt away. A plastic plant is cheap, requires no time or energy and can give you the aesthetic of a real plant without all the work.
